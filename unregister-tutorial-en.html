Basic Rule

There are basic rules that you need to know before you can play Yu-gi-oh. These include standard playmat, deck size limitations and the gameplay mechanics.

Playmat

A Yu-gi-oh Duel is conducted on a playmat, like the one shown below:

At the right corner of players' field is Main Deck Zone - where you place your Main Deck.

At the left corner of players' field is Extra Deck Zone - where you place your Extra Deck.

Above the Main Deck's zone is the Graveyard-GY which is used to contain used card or destroyed cards.

Above the Extra Deck's zone is the Field Zone where you place Field Spell.

Between the Field Zone and GY, there are 5 Main Monster Zones where your monsters are placed and battle.

Between the Extra Deck Zone and the Main Deck Zone, there are 5 Spell & Trap Zones where you place your Spells and Traps. However, the two zones on the far left and right are unique. These are also Pendulum Zones, where you can place Pendulum Monsters.

In the middle of the playmat, there're 2 additional zones called Extra Monster Zone. Most monsters from Extra Deck can be placed in Main Monster Zones, except Link Monster that can only be placed in Extra Monster Zones.

Decks

To start a Yu-gi-oh duel, in addition to the playmat, you need a deck(s):

● Main Deck (must contain 40-60 cards): This deck includes Monsters, Spells and Traps that you will use throughout the duel.

● Extra Deck (can contain 0-15 cards): This deck includes monsters that can be only summoned by special methods. These monsters can make your static more competitive, helping you defeat your opponent more easily.

● Side Deck (can contain 0-15 cards): In standard Match (which consist of 3 Duels), after finishing a Duel, you can add and remove cards from your Main Deck/Extra Deck to deal up with opponent's deck (this usually happens in a tournament). However, remember that your deck size must remain constant after the siding process.

Gameplay

At the start of the Duel, 2 players must decide who will go first (by rolling a dice, rock-paper-scissor, etc...). Then, each players draw 5 cards and player who goes first will start their turn.

Phases In A Turn

In a turn, there are 6 phases:

● Draw Phase: The player draw 1 card from their Main Deck (the player going first doesn't draw in the first turn).

● Standby Phase: The phase that occurs between the Draw Phase and Main Phase 1.

● Main Phase 1: During Main Phase, the player can summon monsters and activate Spells and Traps.

● Battle Phase: During this phase, your monsters can attack and battle with your opponent's monsters (whoever goes first can't conduct Battle Phase).

● Main Phase 2: After the Battle Phase, you can perform actions similar to those in Main Phase 1, such as summoning monsters and activating Spells and Traps.

● End Phase: The end of the turn.

Summon Monster

There are 4 types of Summons in Yu-gi-oh: Normal Summon, Set, Flip Summon and Special Summon:

● Normal Summon: Place your monster from your hand into your Main Monster Zone vertically (face-up).

● Set: Place your monster from your hand into your Main Monster Zone horizontally (face-down).

● Flip Summon: When your monster already Set in the Main Monster Zone, you can flip it up (place it vertically) (You can't Flip Summon a monster in the turn it was set).

● Special Summon: A summon performed using a special method or under special conditions (can be placed face-up, either vertically or horizontally).

You can either Normal Summon or Set a monster once per turn.

You can Flip Summon monsters as many as you want, if you have enough Set monsters.

You can Special Summon monsters as many as you want.

Winning Conditions

● Each players start with 8000 Life Points (LP). The player who can make opponent's LP reduce to 0 wins the Duel, if both player's LP are reduced to 0 at the same time, it will be treated as a Draw.

● While players need to draw, whoever can't draw card loses the Duel, if both, it will be treated as a Draw.

● By the effects of certain cards, if you meet the conditions listed on the card, you can achieve a special win.

Battling Mechanism

Now, let's learn about Battle Position. There 3 types battle position: Attack Postion, face-up Defense Postion and face-down Defense Postion:

● Attack Postion: Monsters that were placed vertically.

● Face-up Defense Postion: Monsters that were placed horizontally (face-up).

● Face-down Defense Postion: Monsters that were Set.

A monster can't attack unless it is in Attack Position. If it attacks a monster, there are 3 situations:

● If attacked monster is in Attack Position: Which monster with the lower ATK will be destroyed (and sent to GY). The owner will lose LP equal to the difference in ATK.

● If attacked monster is in face-up Defense Position: if attacking monster's ATK less than attacked monster's DEF, it won't be destroyed, but the owner still loses LP equal to the difference; if attacked monster has less DEF than attacking monster's ATK, it will be destroyed, but the owner won't lose any LP.

● If attacked monster is in face-down Position: It must first be flipped up, the rules will be apllied like with monster in face-up Defense Position.

Understand The Card

In Yu-gi-oh, we have 3 types of cards: Monster, Spell, Trap.

Monster Card

A Monster Card will include:

● Card Name: Used to identify cards and distinguish them from each other.

● Attribute: There're 7 Attributes - LIGHT, DARK, FIRE, WATER, EARTH, WIND, DIVINE.

● Level (from 1 - 12): Great Level cames with great power.

The Level of monsters decide how they can be Normal Summoned or Set: For Level 4 or lower monsters, you can Normal Summon/Set it without Tributing (send monster from field to GY); for Level 5 or 6 monsters, you must Tributing 1 monster you control to Normal Summon/Set it; for Level 7 or more monsters, you must Tribute 2.

● Card Image: Show monster image.

● Information: Includes monsters types such as Spellcaster, Dragon, Warrior,...

● Description/Effect: To descript about monster and its effects.

● ATK/DEF.

Next, we will learn about some types of monsters.

Normal Monster

Normal Monster is monster card with no effect, so in its card text will be its description.

As you can see, in the information of Normal Monster Card, beside its type is text "Normal"

Effect Monster

Effect Monster is monster card with special effects, which are described in its card text. And in its information is text "Effect"

This card's text says: "Cannot be destroyed by battle with monsters that has 1900 ATK or more". This means that when this monster battles with monster that has 1900 ATK or more, it can be destroyed even though it has lower ATK, but you still lose LP.

A Monster can also be both an Effect Monster and a Ritual, Fusion, Synchro, Xyz, Pendulum or Link Monster

Ritual Monster

Ritual Monster is monster that only can be Special Summoned (from your hand) by using Ritual Spell and requiring tributing monsters from field or hand whose total Levels equal or exceed the Level Ritual Monster. This summon method is called "Ritual Summon" (All monsters must be face-up before being sent to GY).

There are Level 8 Ritual Monster and Ritual Spell used to Ritual Summon it, so the Spell's text will soecify that you must tribute monsters whose total Levels equal 8 or more.

Fusion Monster

Fusion Monster is Extra Deck monster that only can be Special Summoned by Spells like "Polymerization" or "Fusion" and requires sending monster from hand or field that listed in the Fusion Monster's text. This summon method is called "Fusion Summon" (All monster must be face-up before being sent to GY).

This Fusion Monster requires 3 "Blue-eyes White Dragon" mean you need 3 "Blue-eyes White Dragon" in your hand or field to activate Spell "Polymerization" to Fusion Summon it.

Synchro Monster

Synchro Monster is Extra Deck monster that only can be Special Summoned by sending monster from your field to GY whose total Levels exactly equal to the Level of Synchro Monster. This summon method is called "Synchro Summon" (All monsters must be face-up before being sent to GY).

In addition, Synchro Summon requires a specific type of monster - Tuner along with non-Tuner monsters.

In the field, we have Level 3 Tuner Monster and Level 3 non-Tuner monsters, so we can Synchro Summon Level 6 (3+3) Synchro Monster.

Xyz Monster

Xyz Monster is also Extra Deck monster but it is unique because it doesn't have Level. To Xyz Summon it, you need overlay monsters in your field with he same Level in your field, and place Xyz Monster on top of those monsters. (All monsters must be face-up before being overlayed)

Xyz Monster "stars" will place in opposite position with other types of monsters, it called Rank, and the Rank is equal to the Level of overlayed monsters. (For example, Rank 4 Monster is Summoned with Level 4 monsters.)

If Xyz Monster is also an Effect Monster, you can remove its overlayed monsters to GY (detach materials) for activating its effects (but it doesn't count as sending to GY)

For example, opponent's monster declare an attack target your monster, but your monster can negate that attack by detaching its material.

Pendulum Monster

Pendulum Monsters are very special monsters, a combinatioin of Spell and Normal, Effect, Ritual, Fusion, Synchro or Xyz Monster. And, in case Pendulum Monsters would be sent to GY, it will be place on top of Extra Deck instead of that (face-up).

Pendulum Monsters' lower half colored of Spell and upper half colored of Monster card. Also they has extra description-part called "Pendulum Scale" which is important to Pendulum Summon.

Pendulum Monster can still be Summoned like the way to Summon its monster type (Normal, Ritual, Fusion, Synchro, Xyz).

To Pendulum Summon, you must first place 2 Pendulum Monsters in Pendulum Zones. Placing them will count as activating Spell and also these monsters will be treated as Spells (you can only use its effects that are described in Pendulum Scale).

While you having 2 Pendulum Monsters in Pendulum Zones, you can Pendulum Summon monsters. Depending on Pendulum Scales of those 2 Pendulum Monsters, you can Special Summon any number of monsters from (your hand) or Pendulum Monsters that placed face-up on Extra Deck whose Levels is in the range of Pendulum Scale.

For example, if you have 2 Pendulum Monsters: one with Pendulum Scale of 1 and the other with Pendulum Scale of 8, you can Special Summon monsters whose Level from 2 to 7.

You can even Pendulum Summon monsters that are not Pendulum Monster if their Level is in Pendulum Scale. Moreover, you can only Pendulum Summon 1 Pendulum Monster from Extra Deck per turn (and it must be placed on Extra Monster Zone), you also can only Pendulum Summon once per turn.

Link Monster

Link Monster is Extra Deck monsters which can easily Special Summon by sending number of monsters from your field to GY equal to its Link Rating (Link Summon). There are 2 Extra Monster Zones in the field, but you can only use one of them.

Spell Card

Spell Card is a card that you can activate from your hand by placing it in your Spell & Trap Zones to give yourself more advantages. A Spell Card will include:

Continuous Spell

After activating this card, it will remains on the field, and Insect monsters your opponent controls can't declare attack.

Field Spell

Field Spells are the same as Continuous Spell, but is will be placed on Field Zone instead of Spell & Trap Zones.

All of Spell Types above can only be activated during the Main Phase

Quick-Play Spell

Quick-Play Spell is the same as Normal Spell, but you can activate it during any Phases of the turn.

Trap Card

Trap Cards are cards that can be only activate if they were set (face-down horizontally) in the previous turn. (Spells can also be set, but you can activate it the turn it was set except Quick-Play Spell).

A Trap Card will include:

● Card Name: To identify cards with each others.

● Trap Icon.

● Trap Symbol: Normal, Continuous, Counter

Normal Trap

Normal Traps work as Normal Spells and can make create the unprediction in your tactics, will be sent to GY after it resolves.

Continuous Trap

Continuous Traps work as Continuous Spells, will remain on the field forever.

Counter Trap

Counter Traps is a card that can counter your opponent's tactics, will be sent to GY after it resolves.

Understand The Monster Effects

Monster Effects are classified to 4 types: Ignition, Trigger, Continuous or Quick Effect. (Quick Effect is can be either Ignition or Trigger Effect).

Ignition Effect

Ignition Effect is the effect that you can activate it whenever you want during your Main Phases (any Phases if it is Quick Effect).

Trigger Effect

Trigger Effect is the effect that can be only activated when suitable trigger happens (when a monster is summoned, a card is activated,...)

Continuous Effect

Continuous Effect don't need to be activated, also it always available on the field.

Quick Effect

Quick Effect is a effect that can be activated during any Phases of turn.

Spell Speed and Chain Link

Sometimes, there will be 2 or more cards or effects can be activated. So how we know which card can be activated first, and which card will resolve first.

Spell Speed

Spell Speed refers how "fast" a card or effect resolves after activation. There are 3 Spell Speeds:

● Spell Speed 1: The activation of Normal, Equip, Continuous, Field, Ritual Spell, or its effects and the activation of monsters' effects.

Cards or effects has Spell Speed 1 can't be activated in respond to other cards or effects activation, it means you can activated 2 or more cards that have Spell Speed at the same time.

● Spell Speed 2: The activation of Normal, Continuous Traps, Quick-Play Spells or its effects and the activation of monsters' Quick Effects.

Cards or effects has Spell Speed 2 can be activated in respond to Spell Speed 2 and Spell Speed 1 cards or effects activation.

● Spell Speed 3: Only the activations of Counter Traps has Spell Speed 3, of course it can be activated in respond to any other cards or effects activation.

If 2 or more cards are activated in a row, it will create a Chain Link.

Chain Link

When a cards or effects are activated consecutively, the first activated card is Chain Link 1, the second card is Chain Link 2,... Then, card with highest Chain Link will resolve first.
